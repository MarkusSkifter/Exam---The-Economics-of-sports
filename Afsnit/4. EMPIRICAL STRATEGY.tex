\section{Empirical Strategy}

\subsection{Cox Proportional Hazard Model}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Justify the choice of a Cox PH model: semi-parametric, does not require distributing the baseline hazard, handles right-censoring (riders who finish), and naturally models duration dependence.
    \item Write out the hazard function: $h(t \mid \mathbf{X}) = h_0(t) \exp(\mathbf{X} \boldsymbol{\beta})$, and explain interpretation of hazard ratios ($\exp(\beta) > 1$ means higher abandonment hazard).
    \item Explain the covariates used in the basic model: age, experience, previous DNF rate, GC contender, team size, team average age, race dummies, and a centred year trend.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Time-Varying Extension}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Explain why stage-level covariates require a time-varying Cox model: the difficulty of the race changes from stage to stage, and abandonment hazard is not constant within a race.
    \item Describe the additional time-varying covariates: stage type dummies (mountain, hilly, individual TT, team TT vs.\ flat baseline) and standardised stage distance.
    \item Note the counting-process data structure (start-stop intervals) and the use of \texttt{CoxTimeVaryingFitter}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Proportional Hazards Assumption}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Explain the Schoenfeld residual test for the PH assumption.
    \item Report results from Table~4: none of the covariates violate the PH assumption at the 5\% level, which validates the model specification.
    \item Mention the Schoenfeld residual plots (Figure~6) as visual confirmation.
\end{itemize}
